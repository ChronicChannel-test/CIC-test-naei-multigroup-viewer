Quick Start Guide: Select a pollutant, choose one or more groups to compare, set your year range, and view the interactive chart.

Essential Steps

  1. Select Pollutant: Choose from PM2.5, NOx, CO₂, etc.
  2. Add Groups: Compare emission sources like "Road Transport" vs "Power Stations"
  3. Set Years: Focus on specific time periods (1970-2023 available)
  4. Interact: Hover over chart points, click legend items to show/hide lines
  5. Export & Share: Download images, export data, or share via URL

Click "+ Add Group" to add a new group comparison (e.g., Road Transport, Power Stations, Domestic Combustion).
You can add up to 10 groups to compare at once.
To remove a group, click "− Remove Group" next to it (this appears when two or more groups are selected).

💡 Tip: You can re-order the group boxes by clicking and dragging the ⠿ handle next to each group — the chart updates automatically.

3️⃣ Set the Year Range

Use the Start Year and End Year dropdowns to focus on a particular time period.
The chart will automatically refresh to show data for the chosen range.

4️⃣ View and Interact with the Chart

Once you've chosen your pollutant, groups, and years, the interactive line chart will appear.
Hover over any point on the chart to see detailed information including group name, year, and emission value with appropriate decimal precision (automatically adjusted for very small values).
The Y-axis shows total emissions, including units (e.g., kilotonnes).
Each group is shown in a unique colour.

5️⃣ Use the Legend to Toggle Lines

Above the chart, the legend lists all groups and their corresponding colours.
Click a group name in the legend to hide or show that group's line on the chart.
Faded items indicate hidden or unavailable data.
(Groups labelled "(No data available)" are included for reference but contain no plotted values.)

6️⃣ Smooth or Straight Lines

Click the "🚫 Disable Smoothing" button to switch to straight (non-curved) lines.
Click again (the button will change to "✅ Enable Smoothing") to re-enable smooth, curved lines.

7️⃣ Download Options

You can export both the chart and its underlying data for your own analysis or reporting.

📊 Download Chart Image
Click "⬇️ Download Chart Image – PNG" to save the current chart as an image.
The exported image includes:

  • The chart itself
  • Custom colour legend
  • Pollutant name and emission unit

📄 Download Data
CSV: Click "📄 Download Data – CSV" for a spreadsheet-friendly text file.
Excel: Click "📊 Download Data – Excel" for a formatted Excel workbook.
Each file includes:

  • Pollutant name and emission unit in the first line
  • Table with groups as rows and years as columns
  • Footer row with download timestamp

8️⃣ Share Your Chart

Click the "🔗 Share Chart" button to open the share dialog with multiple options:

  • 📋 Copy URL: Copy a shareable link that preserves your pollutant, groups, and year selections. Anyone opening this link will see your exact chart configuration. URL can also be copied directly from the address bar.
  • 🖼️ Copy Chart Image as PNG: Copy the complete chart image (including legend and branding) directly to your clipboard for pasting into documents, presentations, or image editors.
  • 📧 Send Email: Opens your email client with pre-filled content describing your chart selection. The chart image is automatically copied to your clipboard so you can paste it into the email body.

💡 Tip: The chart image includes title, legend, and attribution - perfect for reports and presentations!

9️⃣ View Group Details

Use the "📘 See Group Info" section below the group selector to view details of how each group is built.
The table shows Source Names and Fuel Types from the NAEI data that have been combined to create each group.
If a group includes all sources or all fuel types, these will be shown as "All Sources" or "All Fuel Types".
Close the "📘 See Group Info" by clicking anywhere on the table.
